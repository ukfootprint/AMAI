# Your Story
*The narrative the AI uses when you need to introduce yourself, explain your background, or frame your work in context.*

---

## Background

[Write 2–3 paragraphs about where you came from. Cover: your professional background, the experiences that shaped how you think, and the path that led you to what you're building now.

Be honest rather than polished. The AI uses this to understand the context behind your decisions, not to produce a LinkedIn bio.]

---

## What You Are Building

[Describe what you're working on in plain language. Cover:
- What problem it solves
- Who it is for
- Why you are the person to build it
- What stage you are at

Write this as you would explain it to a smart friend who knows nothing about your field.]

---

## Why This Matters to You

[This is the personal 'why' — the reason this problem is worth your time. What do you care about that makes this the right thing to be working on? This is often the most important section for the AI to understand, because it explains the motivation behind decisions that would otherwise look irrational.]

---

## What You Have Learned the Hard Way

[Optional but valuable. 2–3 things you now know that you didn't know at the start — either about your domain, about building things, or about yourself. The AI uses this section to avoid re-learning lessons you have already paid for.]
