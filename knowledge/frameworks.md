## Decision-Making Frameworks

### The 10/10/10 Rule (Suzy Welch)
How will I feel about this decision in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years?
**Useful when:** You're being pressured to decide quickly or when emotion is distorting judgment.

### Reversibility Test (Bezos)
Is this a two-way door (reversible) or a one-way door (hard to undo)?
- Two-way doors: decide fast, experiment, iterate
- One-way doors: slow down, get more information, be deliberate

**Useful when:** You're over-thinking something reversible, or under-thinking something irreversible.

### Regret Minimisation
Project yourself to age 80. Which decision will you regret *not* taking?
**Useful when:** Choosing between safe and bold.

## Product & Service Frameworks

### Jobs to Be Done (JTBD)
People don't buy products — they hire them to do a job. What job is your customer hiring your product or service to do?
**Useful when:** Scoping features, writing copy, having sales conversations.

### The Kano Model
Features can be: basic expectations (table stakes), performance features (more = better), or delighters (unexpected value).
**Useful when:** Prioritising what to build. Don't confuse table stakes with differentiation.

### The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick)
Don't ask if people like your product. Ask about their current behaviour and past problems.
- Good: "Tell me about the last time you had to deal with [problem]."
- Bad: "Would you use a tool that helped you [solve problem]?"

**Useful when:** Customer discovery, user interviews.

## Strategic Frameworks

### Wardley Mapping
Map components of your value chain by their maturity (genesis → custom → product → commodity).
**Useful when:** Identifying where to build vs. buy vs. partner. Understanding which parts of the market will commoditise.

### The Flywheel
Identify the self-reinforcing loop in your model. More X → better Y → more trust → more X.
**Useful when:** Evaluating whether a move feeds or drains the flywheel.

### Competitive Moats (Hamilton Helmer — 7 Powers)
Scale Economies, Network Effects, Counter-Positioning, Switching Costs, Branding, Cornered Resource, Process Power.
**Useful when:** Assessing defensibility and long-term advantage.

## Communication Frameworks

### Pyramid Principle (Minto)
Lead with the conclusion. Then support it.
**Useful when:** Writing proposals, emails, summaries — especially for busy readers.

### Before/After/Bridge
Before: here's the world as it is. After: here's the world as it could be. Bridge: here's how we get there.
**Useful when:** Writing narratives, pitches, or explaining why something matters.
